WILL MIRA SEE
THE DAWN?

The greenhouse child, nine years old, keeper of the city’s last tomato vines, was named by the Long Dark at twelve o’clock. The city has until first light to answer.

By quiet_marrow, City Desk  ·  Day 5, 11:47 p.m.

MIRA of the glasshouse — nine years old, sixty pounds in wet boots, custodian of the only tomatoes left on this side of the ash line — was Marked at the midnight toll. The bell rang once, and every lantern in the south ward went out, and when they were lit again her name was on the board.

The Council does not choose the Marked. The Dark does. What the city chooses is whether to answer. Five hundred pledges before first light and the vigil holds; fewer, and the Gazette prints her name tomorrow in a black border, as it printed the miller’s, and the bell-ringer’s, and the name of the man who fixed the pumps.

“She waters the vines at six and talks to them,” said the night warden, who would not give a name. “You want to know what we’re saving? That. That exactly.” Pledges are taken at the coupons below, and are counted twice at the count table.

Day 5 · 11:52 p.m. · The Glasshouse Urgent

The child Mira was Marked at the midnight toll. The board was chalked before the echo died. Vigil forms at the glasshouse door; the count table is open on the front page. She has asked, twice, who will water the vines tomorrow. Nobody has answered her yet.

— filed by quiet_marrow, city desk
Day 5 · 11:14 p.m. · East Gate

Howling past the tree line, nearer than Tuesday. The watch counts “more than ten, fewer than a hundred, and moving.” The gate question goes to ballot tonight — mark your box before first light or hold your peace at the funerals.

— filed by ashen_fox, watchman, east parapet
Day 5 · 9:40 p.m. · Council Hall Council Minute

Ration amendment carried, seven votes to two. Bread at three-quarter weight until the flour convoy; children and the fever ward exempt. Councillor Brask dissenting, loudly, and then at length, and then alone.

— recorded by brackenwren, clerk of session
Day 5 · 8:03 p.m. · Fever Ward

The ward has taken seven more since nightfall and will not hold past Friday unless physic arrives from saltmarsh by the shore road. The sisters ask for boiled linen and quiet. They are getting neither.

— filed by sable_reed, orderly
Day 5 · 6:30 p.m. · The Presses

For the record: the north wall stands because four hundred eighty-seven of you signed a coupon and then actually came. The masons worked by lantern; the pledge line stretched past the bakery. Yesterday’s edition is already trading above cover price. We print more hope tonight; bring your own ink if you have any.

— set by palewick, pressman
Day 5 · 5:12 p.m. · Skywatch

Ash front moving in from the ashlands, expected over the city by noon tomorrow. Cover the cisterns tonight. The sunset, for what it is worth, was the colour of a struck match, and lasted eleven minutes.

— filed by saltcedar, skywatch
Day 5 · 4:47 p.m. · The Wire Room

Intercept, r/ironhollow to all stations: they ask after our dynamo design and offer ore. The Council debates whether an answer makes us allies or a target. The operator notes that Pinevale never answered anybody, either.

— logged by coldharbor, wire operator
Day 5 · 4:02 p.m. · South Ward

The goat Turnip, missing since the collapse of pen four, was discovered inside the chapel eating a hymnal. Morale in the south ward is reported “measurably improved.” The hymnal was Psalms. The goat is unrepentant.

— filed by lastferry, south ward
Day 5 · 3:30 p.m. · The Archive

A correction: Day 4’s edition gave the miller’s name as Aldous. It was Aldric. He kept the stones turning for thirty years and we misspelled him in his black border. The Gazette regrets this more than it can print.

— filed by brackenwren, for the editors
